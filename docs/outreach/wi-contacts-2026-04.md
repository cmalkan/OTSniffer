- **SFY 2027 is the live window for Q2–Q4 2026 outreach:**
  - SDWLP **ITA deadline was 2025-10-31** (if a utility hasn't submitted an Initial Technical Assistance intake yet, they're likely outside the 2027 principal-forgiveness priority track — this changes the sales talk from "help you submit" to "help you build evidence for the next cycle or a non-priority pathway").
  - **SDWLP complete-application deadline for principal forgiveness: 2026-06-30** for the SFY starting 2026-07-01. **This is the active outreach window right now.** Day 3 email's "grant-eligibility language" attachment is directly applicable.
  - CWFP equivalent deadline follows a similar fall submission pattern — verify on CF0046b deadlines PDF before citing.

### Recent Wisconsin cyber-posture signals

- **EPA enforcement pivot (May 2024, updated July 2025).** EPA has taken 100+ enforcement actions nationally under SDWA §1433 since 2020, with civil penalties up to $69,733/day. No *public* WI-specific enforcement letter was located in this pass — but EPA Region 5 (which covers WI) has been active nationally, and the Day 3 email's "peer precedent" language is honest if framed as *"EPA Region 5 and adjacent-state enforcement"* rather than claiming a named WI incident.
- **No verified WI water-utility ransomware incident** in 2024–2025 searches. The American Water (Oct 2024) incident is federal-scope and touches WI indirectly if at all; do not cite it as a WI incident in outreach.
- **WI Cyber Badger 2026** (UW School of Computer, Data & Information Sciences cyber exercise) is a state-level cyber awareness signal but not a utility-specific enforcement event.
- **WIWARN (WI Water/Wastewater Agency Response Network)** is the state's WARN mutual-aid network; free membership. WI DNR actively promotes it. Worth a name-check in Day 0 as "we're familiar with WIWARN" — it reads as local-aware.
- **WI SLCGP (CISA grant, via WEM / DET)** Cycle 2 FY2023 Round 2 **closed 2026-01-30**. Utilities are eligible applicants. A utility that didn't apply this round may be planning for the next round — relevant Day 3 talking point.

### Things that might change the outreach approach

1. **Leadership transition at Waukesha Water Utility.** Dan Duchniak retired June 2025 and the successor is not publicly named at 2026-04-20. Transitions = intake window. Reprioritize Waukesha to **row 1** for first outreach — a new GM will have a 90-day listening posture and an urgent AWIA recertification looming.
2. **Marty Pollard (WI DNR) is the single highest-leverage contact on this list.** One good 20-minute conversation with him about Evidence Pack methodology gets Malkan into his resource-guidance rolodex, which scales across the entire 576-utility Wisconsin universe with zero ongoing marketing spend. He is not a buyer — he is a referrer. Treat accordingly.
3. **No dominant MSSP footprint detected in WI water specifically.** Dragos / Claroty / Nozomi coverage is likely concentrated in the 3 largest utilities (Milwaukee, Madison, Green Bay) — which we are deliberately avoiding. In the 15k–80k tier, the threat landscape appears under-served, consistent with Pearl's JTBD hypothesis. This is favorable.
4. **Combined electric + water utilities (Manitowoc, Wausau) have richer OT footprints** than water-only utilities. Our $7,500 Evidence Pack scope may be too narrow to fully serve them — but **they are unusually good targets for a Tier 2 / Impact Map upsell conversation**. Flag for Pearl.
5. **Purchased-water utilities (West Allis, Waukesha post-diversion)** have a fundamentally different OT threat model — distribution + SCADA + PRV controls but no treatment PLCs. Our secrets + supply-chain scanners still apply; the "safety-critical PLC" reassurance language in Day 0 needs a minor rewording for these ("we exclude any pressure-reducing valve or distribution PLC from active probing").
6. **WIAWWA's 2026-05-07 Technology & Security Seminar** is a high-value channel event. If we can get the section's program chair to let the Malkan Sender Name do a 20-minute "what we're seeing" slot, that seeds 50+ utility contacts in one afternoon. Pursue via `contact@wiawwa.org` after verifying the seminar program chair.
