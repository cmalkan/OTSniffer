# JTBD Discovery Interview Guide — Wisconsin Water Utilities

**Author:** Pearl (product) · **Date:** 2026-04-26 · **Target:** 10–12 mid-size Wisconsin water utility GMs / ops directors · **Cadence:** 30 min, no-pitch · **Window:** Tue/Thu 9–11 + 2–4 local, starting W3 (2026-05-05)

> **Why this exists.** Sales motion is paused. Before resuming Day 0 sends with a $7,500 Evidence Pack anchor, we need to confirm Evidence Pack maps to a real, named, recurring job — not to a job we wish existed. This guide is for listening, not pitching. Output: a go/kill/pivot decision on T1 by 2026-05-29.

---

## 1. Purpose & framing

**What we're trying to learn:** does a mid-size Wisconsin water utility GM have a recurring, dollar-backed job that Evidence Pack (passive 1-week OT exposure snapshot, redacted PDF + 30-day dashboard, $7,500 list) actually performs better than what they have today?

**"Yes, there's a job here" signals (we want ≥3 across the interview):**
- Names a specific moment in the last 12 months when they were asked for OT cyber evidence and didn't have it ready (insurer questionnaire, board/council, EPA letter, AWIA RRA refresh, SRF application).
- Describes a current workaround that costs real money or real calendar time (e.g., "we paid $40k for a one-off assessment," "our IT director burned three weeks pulling something together").
- Has a budget *line* (operating, SRF, SLCGP, insurance offset) — not a hypothetical "we could probably find money."
- Names another person who would have to approve, and what that person would need to see.

**"No job, kill the tier" signals (any one is a yellow flag; two = red):**
- Cyber is "an IT problem" and stays inside IT — OT side never sees the question.
- AWIA RRA is treated as a one-time check-the-box exercise; no recurring evidence ask.
- They already have an MSSP / Dragos partner / regional SI on retainer who handles "all of that" and they have no complaint.
- They cannot name a single moment in the last 12 months when OT cyber evidence was asked of them.
- Every cyber dollar goes to insurance premium, not to assessment.

**We may discover Evidence Pack solves no real job.** That is a valid outcome. Pivot options if so: (a) collapse T1 into a free lead-in for T2 Impact Map; (b) reposition as an MSSP-channel-only product; (c) park water and re-test in electric utilities (NERC CIP buyer has a different evidence cadence). Do not paper over a null result with "they just didn't get it."

## 2. Opening (90 seconds — read aloud, do not improvise)

> "Thanks for the 30 minutes. I'm Chetan Malkan — I run a small OT cybersecurity practice called Malkan Solutions. I'm not selling anything today and I won't ask you to commit to anything at the end of this call. I'm in the middle of figuring out whether a service I've prototyped is actually worth building, and the only honest way to answer that is to talk to ten or so utilities of your size and listen to how you actually handle OT cyber evidence today.
>
> I'll ask about your last 12 months — what's been asked of you, what's worked, what's been a slog. There are no right answers. I'll take notes. Nothing you say will be quoted or shared without your written permission. If at any point you want to stop or skip a question, just say so."

**Then ask permission once:** *"Is it OK if I record audio just for my notes? I'll delete it after I write the transcript."* If no, don't record. Take handwritten notes.

**Disclose, do not pitch:**
- "I'm exploring whether to build a one-week OT cyber evidence service. I haven't decided whether to build it."
- "I'm not asking you to be a customer. I'm asking for your perspective."
- Do **not** name the tiers. Do **not** say $7,500. Do **not** describe the deliverable.

## 3. Anti-pitch handling (exact language)

If they ask **"Are you trying to sell me something?"**

> "Fair question. Not today. I genuinely don't know yet whether what I'm building is useful. If it turns out it is, and you'd want to talk later, I'll follow up — but the value of this 30 minutes to me is your honest answer, not a sale. If I tried to sell you right now I'd be doing my own homework wrong."

If they ask **"What's it cost?" / "How much would something like that be?"**

> "Honestly, I'm trying not to anchor you with a number — once I tell you a price the rest of this call becomes a negotiation instead of a conversation. Can I tell you after we wrap, if you still want to know? I'll send the price sheet in the follow-up email."

If they ask **"Can you show me the tool / a sample report?"**

> "I'd rather not on this call — same reason. Once I show you an artifact, you start telling me what you think of *that* instead of what you actually do today. If we end the call and you want me to send the sample, I will. Right now I'm more interested in how you handled [whatever they just described]."

If they push back hard, send the one-pager **after** the call, not during. The interview is not the place to re-open the sales motion.

## 4. The 12 questions

Ordering matters: warm-up first, struggling-moment next, forces last. Ask follow-ups; do not skip ahead. Mom Test rule — past tense, specific, no hypotheticals.

**Warm-up (set the scene)**

1. *"Walk me through your role — what's a typical week look like, and where does cyber sit on your desk versus your IT director's or someone else's?"*
2. *"How big is your system — population served, treatment plants, lift stations? And roughly how old is the OT gear running it?"*

**The struggling moment (the heart of the interview)**

3. *"Tell me about the last time someone — your insurer, the EPA, your board, a council member, anybody — asked you a question about OT cybersecurity that you couldn't answer in the moment. What was the question, and what did you have to do to get them an answer?"*
   - Listen for: who asked, how fast they needed it, what the workaround was, what it cost in dollars or hours.
4. *"Walk me through the last AWIA Risk and Resilience Assessment cycle. What was useful about it, what was a checkbox, and is anyone still using the document a year later?"*
5. *"Has your utility had any OT cyber scare or near-miss in the last 24 months — even something that turned out to be nothing? What happened, and what did you wish you'd had on hand at that moment?"*

**Forces around buying / the hire decision**

6. *"When the utility has spent money on cyber in the last two years — assessment, software, monitoring, training, anything — who proposed it, who approved it, and what made it actually go through versus get cut?"*
   - Pull / push / anxiety / habit. Listen for who pays, who blocks, what kills budget.
7. *"What's gotten a cyber line item killed at your utility? What was the killing argument?"*
8. *"If your insurer sent you a renewal questionnaire next month asking for documented evidence of OT segmentation and exposure, what would you do in the first 48 hours?"*

**The hire criteria (what would they "fire" their current approach for?)**

9. *"Imagine you had to walk into a board or council meeting next quarter and convince them to keep — or grow — the cyber budget. What artifact, document, or piece of evidence would change that conversation?"*
   - This is the load-bearing question. Listen for whether they describe something Evidence Pack actually produces (redacted PDF, regulatory cross-reference, dashboard) or something else entirely (a CISO advisor, a tabletop, a vendor logo).
10. *"If a peer utility GM told you 'I bought X last quarter, best money I spent on cyber all year' — what would X have to be for you to actually call them and ask about it?"*

**Channel & funding (how money moves)**

11. *"Where does cyber money come from at your utility today — operating budget, SRF, SLCGP, insurance offset, grant pass-through, MSSP retainer? And which of those is easiest versus hardest to actually unlock?"*
12. *"Who else should I be talking to — inside your utility, or another utility your size — to understand this better?"*
    - Door-opener for warm intros. Always ask. Always.

## 5. Listening cues — signal vs theater

Capture verbatim. Quotes drive the synthesis; paraphrases lose the signal.

**Real-pain signals (write these down word for word):**

- *"We don't actually know what's on our network."*
- *"Our IT director did a spreadsheet last time and we haven't touched it since."*
- *"The insurer asked and I had to call our integrator to make something up."*
- *"I have $X budgeted but I don't know what to spend it on."*
- *"The board asked me last meeting and I dodged it."*
- *"Our last assessment was [vendor] and it was a [PDF / binder] and nobody opened it again."*
- Any specific dollar figure tied to a specific event.

**Compliance-theater / no-job signals:**

- *"We passed our AWIA RRA, we're good."* (Treats compliance as terminal state.)
- *"That's [IT director's] department, you'd have to ask him."* (Wrong buyer; need to re-route, not pitch.)
- *"We've got [MSSP] and they handle everything."* (No surface area — unless followed by a complaint.)
- *"We'd love to but there's no budget."* (Often true; verify against Q11 — if they can't name *any* funding source, it's a flag, not a price objection.)
- *"Cyber is on our roadmap for [next year / when we hire someone]."* (Future-tense everything = no current job.)
- Long pauses on Q3 / Q5 / Q9 — if they cannot name a moment, the moment doesn't exist for them.

**Yellow flags worth probing:**

- They name AWIA but not insurer or board → job may exist but is narrower than we framed; T1 might still fit, but the grant-eligibility framing is the load-bearing pitch, not blast-radius.
- They describe a workaround they're proud of → Evidence Pack is competing with internal pride, not with a vendor. Hard sell.

## 6. Decision tree — 5 minutes after each interview

Before the next call, before any other work, answer three questions in writing in the tracking sheet. Do not skip.

**Q1 — Job present?**
- *Yes:* They named a specific moment in the last 12 months where OT cyber evidence was asked of them and they struggled to produce it.
- *No:* They could not.
- *Maybe:* They named something adjacent (compliance, insurance) but the struggle wasn't real or wasn't recent.

**Q2 — Does the job map to T1 / T2 / T3 / T4?**
- *T1 (Evidence Pack):* They want a one-shot artifact for a single ask (insurer, board, EPA, SRF app). One-week, one-document, defensible.
- *T2 (Impact Map):* They want to defend a *decision* — rate case, budget, remediation plan. They need attack-path narrative, not just findings.
- *T3 (Proven Pathways):* They want lab-validated proof for an underwriter or regulator. Rare in water; flag if it appears.
- *T4 (Posture Watch):* They want recurring quarterly evidence refresh tied to board cadence.
- *None of the above:* The job exists but Evidence Pack doesn't perform it. Capture what does.

**Q3 — Disposition: kill, pivot, or deepen?**
- *Deepen:* Job is present, maps to a tier, willingness-to-pay signal exists. Schedule a follow-up after interview block closes.
- *Pivot:* Job is present but maps to a tier we mis-positioned (e.g., they want T4 cadence, we led with T1 one-shot). Note the re-package and continue interviews.
- *Kill:* No job, or job exists but is owned/solved elsewhere with no complaint. Move on. Do not retry.

Tag every interview with a one-line job statement in the form: *"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."*

## 7. Synthesis rubric — across 10 interviews

Run synthesis once 10 interviews are complete (target: 2026-05-29). Do not synthesize partial data — small-n premature synthesis kills more pivots than it saves.

**"Validated — resume sales motion" thresholds (all four must hold):**

1. **Job density:** ≥7/10 named a specific recurring moment in the last 12 months that maps to T1, T2, or T4. (T3 not required for water validation.)
2. **Tier-fit concentration:** ≥5/10 of those named jobs map to a *single* tier — clear front door — not scattered across all four.
3. **Willingness-to-pay signal:** ≥4/10 either (a) named a dollar figure they've spent on a comparable workaround in the last 24 months, or (b) named a funding source (SRF, SLCGP, insurance offset, opex line) they could pull from for a one-week assessment. Stated thresholds matter more than enthusiasm.
4. **Channel viability:** ≥3/10 either offered a warm intro to a peer (Q12) OR named an MSSP/SI/insurer who would credibly forward the artifact. Cold-only economics don't close the math at our capacity.

**If all four clear → resume sales motion** with the tier that won the concentration test as the wedge. Update positioning + outreach copy to use the verbatim language from the interviews. Re-stage Day 0 sends.

**If 1–2 fail → pivot, do not relaunch:**
- Job density fails (<7/10): the wedge is wrong. Run a second 5-interview round in a different vertical (electric utilities — different evidence cadence under NERC CIP) before re-investing in water.
- Tier concentration fails (jobs scatter): re-package. T1 may need to fold into a T2-led offer, or split into AWIA-specific vs insurer-specific variants. Re-test with a smaller landing-page experiment, not another 10 interviews.
- Willingness-to-pay fails (<4/10 named dollars or funding): the job is real but the buyer isn't economic. Re-route up — talk to CFOs, insurers, or state SRF administrators directly, not GMs.
- Channel fails (<3/10): cold-only is the path. Re-cost the GTM at our 10 hrs/wk and consider whether to defer launch until a channel partner is in hand.

**Fuzzy data — what to do:**

If interviews come back fuzzy (people are friendly but say nothing specific; can't name moments; "interesting question, I'd have to think about it"), the issue is usually **wrong-buyer** or **wrong-question**, not absent demand. Next experiments, in order:
1. Re-run 3 interviews with operations directors only (skip GMs) — operators feel the pain GMs delegate.
2. Re-run 3 interviews with insurance brokers serving water utilities — different angle on the same job.
3. Run a $200 landing-page test (one-pager + email capture) targeting WIAWWA seminar attendees post 2026-05-07 — falsifies whether the language lands without a synchronous call.

Do not re-run the same questions on the same buyer type expecting different answers.

## 8. What we will NOT do

- **No sample PDF reveal during the call.** Even if asked. Send post-call only, and only if they request it twice.
- **No price discussion.** No tier names. No "$7,500" anchor said aloud.
- **No commitment ask at the end.** No "would you be a pilot customer," no "can I send you an SOW," no "would you take a discounted version." End with: *"Thank you. If I follow up in a few weeks with what I've learned, is that OK?"*
- **No demo of `otsniff` or the dashboard.** Listening, not validating-via-pitch.
- **No leading questions.** Avoid "Do you wish you had X?" Always ask "What did you do?" / "What happened?"
- **No quoting other utilities by name** during the call, even anonymously — they'll pattern-match and the rest of the interview becomes confirmation theater.
- **No skipping the decision tree.** 5 minutes after every call. Cold notes 24 hours later are useless.
