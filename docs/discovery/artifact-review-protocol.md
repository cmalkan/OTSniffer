# Evidence Pack — Artifact Review Protocol

**Owner:** Pearl (product) · **Coordinator:** Amber · **Sponsor capacity:** ~10 hrs/wk
**Artifact under review:** `data/t1-sample.pdf` (5-page Evidence Pack sample, energy-plant fixture)
**Window:** W3–W4 (one calendar week, 5 reviewers, ~10 hrs total)
**Frame:** Mom Test discovery + Kano-style classification of findings. **No selling.**

---

## 1. Goal & success criteria

**Question we are answering:** Does the Evidence Pack artifact tell a water-OT expert something they would *act on*, or is it shelfware dressed in cyan?

**Reviewer pool:** 5 expert peers (DNR cyber lead, AWWA chapter chair, ex-utility CIO, OT-MSSP partner manager, regional SI engineer). Sample size is qualitative — we are looking for convergent signal, not a survey.

### Pass / hold / kill thresholds

| Outcome | Threshold (across 5 reviewers) | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Value confirmed (ship as-is)** | ≥4/5 say findings are real and non-noise · ≥3/5 say "I would hand this to my board / underwriter / GM" · ≥3/5 name a specific person they'd forward it to | Evidence Pack tier holds at $7.5k anchor; outreach can resume |
| **Value conditional (revise)** | 3/5 say findings are real BUT ≥3/5 flag a structural gap (e.g., "no SCADA-side narrative," "no dosing/RTU coverage," "regulatory map is wrong") | Re-cut artifact (water fixture, restructured findings) before any further outreach |
| **Value rejected (rebuild or kill T1)** | ≥3/5 say "I'd skim and shelf" OR ≥3/5 say "I've seen this from MSSP X already" OR ≥4/5 cannot name an action they'd take from it | T1 as currently shaped is not the wedge. Stop selling it. Pivot to T2-led or rebuild from a different finding spine. |

### Cohort definition

All 5 reviewers must be (a) currently or recently embedded in water-OT or adjacent OT delivery, (b) reading on behalf of a buyer they could plausibly name, (c) under NDA only if they request it (default: redacted sample, no NDA needed).

## 3. Review session script (30 minutes)

Mom Test rules: **past tense, no pitching, no hypotheticals about purchase.** Reviewer talks ~70% of the call.

### Opening (3 min)

> "Thanks for the time. To be clear up front: I'm not selling you anything. I'm not asking you to introduce me to a buyer. I want your honest read on whether this artifact would be useful to someone in your world — and I'd rather hear it's not useful now than after I've shipped it to ten utilities. I'm going to ask you to walk me through your reaction page by page. Anything you would have shelved on first read, please say so. The goal is to break this, not bless it. OK to record audio for my own notes? I won't share or quote you without permission."

### Page-by-page walk (18 min — ~3 min/page)

For each page:

1. **First reaction (10 sec).** "What's the first thing your eye lands on?"
2. **What jumps out as useful?** (silence — let them fill it)
3. **What's missing?** (silence again)
4. **Who in your world would you forward this to — name a person, not a role?**
5. **What would they do Monday morning with it?** (this is the action question)

Specific page prompts to layer in:

- **Cover / executive summary:** "If a GM saw only this page, would they keep reading or set it down?"
- **Findings page:** "Are these findings real or noise? Which one would you challenge?"
- **Regulatory cross-reference:** "Does the AWIA / CISA CPG mapping land, or does it read as filler?"
- **Evidence-graded section:** "Does the redaction + hash framing buy you anything, or is it theater?"
- **Recommendations / next-step page:** "Is there a next step here, or is it a dead end?"

### Domain-fit prompts (4 min)

> "This sample was generated against an energy/turbine plant fixture, not a water utility. Where do the findings or framing feel wrong for water? What pattern would you expect to see — SCADA-Wonderware, RTUs at lift stations, chemical-dosing PLCs, historian — that isn't here? If I rebuilt this on a water-flavored fixture, what would change?"

### Closing (5 min)

> 1. "On a scale of 1–5, how much of this is signal vs. noise?"
> 2. "Is there anything I should have asked you that I didn't?"
> 3. "If I came back in three months with a revised version, would you look at it again?"
> 4. "Anyone else you think I should run this past — name and intro optional?"

End with: thank-you, send the 1-page market summary within 2 weeks, no follow-up unless invited.

## 4. Structured feedback form

Filled in by Pearl during/after the call — **not** sent to the reviewer to fill out. We capture in our voice, citing their words.

### 4.1 Page-by-page traffic light

| Page | Red (cut/rebuild) | Yellow (revise) | Green (keep) | Reviewer quote |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Cover / exec summary | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ | |
| 2 — Findings overview | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ | |
| 3 — Evidence detail | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ | |
| 4 — Regulatory cross-ref | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ | |
| 5 — Recommendations | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ | |

### 4.2 Per-finding scoring (Kano-flavored)

For each finding presented in the sample:

| Finding ID | Would-act | Would-shelf | Would-question | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| F-1 | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ | |
| F-2 | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ | |
| ... | | | | |

- **Would-act** = "I'd take this to someone Monday." (performance attribute)
- **Would-shelf** = "Interesting, file it." (indifferent)
- **Would-question** = "I'd push back on the methodology or severity." (reverse / must-have failure)

### 4.3 Must-add list

Verbatim list of things the reviewer said are missing. No editorializing. Stack-rank by frequency across all 5 reviewers in synthesis.

### 4.4 Kill list

Verbatim list of things the reviewer said are noise, filler, or wrong. Stack-rank.

### 4.5 Tertiary signal — would they pay?

**We do not ask this directly.** We capture it only if the reviewer volunteers it. Examples to log: "I'd buy this," "my budget would cover this," "this is grant-eligible at our place," "we already pay X for this." Tertiary because purchase intent from a peer reviewer is unreliable signal — Path A (real buyer outreach) owns purchase-intent measurement.

### 4.6 Primary signal — would they recommend it?

Captured from two questions in the closing:

- "Name a person you'd forward this to." (specific person = strong signal · role only = weak · "no one comes to mind" = kill signal)
- "Anyone else I should run this past?" (warm intro offered = strong signal · names without intro = medium · "not really" = neutral, not negative)

## 5. Synthesis rubric

After all 5 sessions, Pearl produces a 1-page synthesis answering five questions in this exact order:

1. **Are findings real or noise?** Pass = ≥4/5 say "real." Fail at any other ratio.
2. **Would they hand it to a named human?** Pass = ≥3/5 name a specific person (board, GM, underwriter, ops director). Soft pass = 2/5 name + 2/5 role. Fail = ≤1/5 name.
3. **Is the regulatory framing load-bearing or filler?** Pass = ≥3/5 say it lands · Fail = ≥3/5 say it's filler.
4. **Is this differentiated from MSSP partner deliverables?** Pass = ≤1/5 say "I've seen this from Dragos/Claroty/Nozomi already." Fail = ≥2/5 say it duplicates.
5. **Does the energy-fixture mismatch invalidate the read?** Pass = ≤2/5 say the mismatch made the artifact unreadable. Fail = ≥3/5 say "I couldn't evaluate this for water."

**Decision matrix:**

- **All 5 pass → ship.** Resume Path A outreach with current artifact.
- **4/5 pass with #5 failing → regenerate against water fixture, no other changes, re-test with 2 reviewers.**
- **3/5 pass → conditional revise.** Pearl + Amber meet, scope a single rebuild cycle, re-test before any further utility outreach.
- **≤2/5 pass → kill or rebuild T1 spine.** Evidence Pack as currently shaped is not the wedge.

## 6. Adversarial questions to seed

Three pre-loaded self-critiques. If the reviewer doesn't raise them spontaneously, Pearl asks them in the closing 5 minutes. Hearing the pushback now is cheaper than hearing it from a buyer.

1. **"What's in this report that the utility's own SCADA integrator couldn't tell them in a 1-hour walk-down?"** — tests whether the artifact's value is inside the SI's free zone.
2. **"If a Dragos partner sent me a quickstart deliverable for the same scope, what would be in theirs that's not in this one — and what's in this one that's not in theirs?"** — tests channel differentiation directly.
3. **"What's the legal or insurance risk to a utility of putting their name on a report from a one-person LLC with $1M E&O? Would your GC sign off on receiving this?"** — tests the credibility ceiling that solo-shop status imposes.

## 7. What we will NOT do

- **No price reveal.** $7,500 anchor is not mentioned, not in email, not on the call, not in the artifact footer if it currently appears there (verify before sending).
- **No commitment ask.** No "would you buy this," no "would your utility be a pilot," no "could we send this to your members."
- **No introduction request during the session.** If a reviewer offers an intro, accept gratefully. We do not ask.
- **No purchase-intent question.** Purchase intent is measured by Path A (real buyer outreach with real SOWs). Mixing peer review with sales contaminates both signals.
- **No defending the artifact.** When a reviewer says "this is wrong," the response is "say more" — not "well, the reason we did it this way is…" That's a pitch tell.
- **No quoting the reviewer to other reviewers.** Each session is independent; cross-pollination biases later sessions.

## 8. Energy-vs-water mismatch caveat

**The fixture problem.** `data/plant-enriched.json` is currently an energy/turbine plant. The sample PDF therefore shows turbine-flavored asset names, vendor patterns (likely GE / Emerson / Honeywell turbine-side rather than Wonderware / Schneider / Allen-Bradley water-side), and finding shapes that won't match what a Wisconsin water-utility reviewer expects to see.

**Patterns the sample WILL NOT show that water reviewers expect:**

- SCADA on Wonderware / iFIX / GE Cimplicity at the master site
- RTUs at remote lift stations / well sites / pressure-reducing valves, often via cellular or licensed radio
- Chemical-dosing PLCs (chlorine, fluoride, phosphate) with safety implications
- Historian (typically OSIsoft PI or AVEVA) with regulatory reporting feeds
- Vendor remote-access pathways for SCADA integrators (the #1 real-world entry point)

**Protocol instruction:** Every reviewer hears the explicit prompt in §3 ("Domain-fit prompts") asking them to flag where the energy fixture breaks the read. Their answers feed synthesis question #5.

**Recommendation on regeneration:** See top-of-file return summary. Default position is **regenerate before showing to Pollard or AWWA chair**, ship as-is to the other three.

## 9. Operating cadence (Amber lane)

Per-reviewer time budget: ~2 hrs (15 min outreach + 30 min prep + 30 min session + 30 min synthesis-capture + 15 min thank-you note). 5 × 2 = 10 hrs = sponsor's full week.

Sequencing:

- **Day 1 (Mon):** All 5 outreach emails sent. AWWA name-surfacing email sent first (longest lead).
- **Day 2–3 (Tue–Wed):** Schedule sessions as replies land. Slot SI and ex-CIO first (lowest stakes, calibrate the script).
- **Day 4 (Thu):** First two sessions. Pearl revises script in-flight if a question consistently misfires.
- **Day 5 (Fri):** Remaining sessions. Pollard and AWWA chair go last — by then the script is hardened and the water-fixture decision is informed by the SI's read.
- **Weekend:** Synthesis written. 1-page market summary drafted for reviewer giveback.
- **Week+1 Mon:** Synthesis reviewed with Amber. Decision: ship / revise / kill. Market summary sent to all 5 reviewers same day.
